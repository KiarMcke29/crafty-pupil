What you need

There are a view items you need to get started in your journey into the digital word of art!

Drawing tablet or iPad

  Drawing on a screen is an essential part of visual art. There are drawing tablets the range from a price as low as $27.99 to as high as $3,499

  Depending on your preference and budget, find a tablet most suitable for you. Most beginners get a cheap tablet with no screen. These tablets are fairly easy to use and can be hooked up to a laptop or desktop. You'll be able to view your movement and art piece on the screen of the device it's hooked up to. On the other hand, some digital artists prefer a tablet with a screen because it feels more realistic. You also hook this tablet to a laptop or desktop and it acts like a second monitor, so you can view your art on two devices. Although these tablets are a little more expensive ranging from $299 to $600.

  A drawing tablet doesn't always have to be the door to the digital world. You can also use an iPad! Those ignorant to drawing tablets normally start on their mobile device or an iPad. If you already have an iPad, I'd recommend you start drawing digitally on that. Download a drawing app through the App store, buy a stylus, and begin drawing. Although it's very convenient for traveling purposes, if you don't have an iPad already, I'd strongly recommend starting with a drawing tablet. They're a lot cheaper and beginner friendly.

The Platform

Where you're drawing is also an important step. There are well known programs like Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Illustrate. Although those are great art applications, not everyone has the money to pay for Adobe. Here are some options for those who want to make digital art for free!

  • ibis Paint
  • MediBang Paint
  • Clip Studio Paint
There are plenty more free platforms to draw on, but these are well known for being beginner friendly and free!
