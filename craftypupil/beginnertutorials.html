Practice makes perfect!

No matter the style you draw in, practicing is always important. I'd recommend picking up a paper and pencil. It's best to practice on what feels the most natural. Of course the median you pick is completely up to you!

"Learn the rules before you break them", applies to everything! Whether you draw cartoons,anime style, semi-realism, or realism; learning how the natural body is shaped and how it moves will give a realistic look to your art. Take time to learn where the ears are placed, how male legs are shaped, and how the body looks when it runs. Do these anatomy practices often while also practicing your art style and watch how much your art improves!


If you're interested in getting a physical book to be your guide, try the "Morpho" series, Michel Lauricella. These small cheap books give you a step-by-step guide to creating your figure drawing.

Create a Practice schedule

It's best when you're deciding to practice you create a schedule so that you have enough time TO practice. Schedules are important for organization. Here's a good example of a well organized schedule for practicing:

Sunday Monday Tuesday Wednesday Thursday Friday Saturday
Practice your weakest ability Practice proportions Practice bodies in motion Practice hands and feet Break Practice facial expressions Practice your art style
